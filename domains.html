Domain Names

For users on the internet, the domain is used to identify and access the website of a specific organization through the URL. For example, if you want to access the NJIT homepage, you would type "NJIT.edu" in the URL, in which your device will specifically locate NJIT's website by its domain name and it's top level domain (.edu, since it is a higher level educational institute.) All websites have specific domains for users to identify and access without having to type or memorize the strings of numbers that accompany the IP addresses.

ICANN

The Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers, ICANN is a regulatory organization that manages the use and issuing of domains on the internet. While the internet is not owned or operated by any one nation's government, this group acts as an independent oversight for preserving free and fair usage of domain access for individuals and organizations all around the world who wish to gain an internet domain.

"By no means is this an easy mission to accomplish. ICANN has to work with every government on the planet to ensure that domain usage is standardized and this means having to negotiate contracts with each of these countries. This is an ambitious undertaking that keeps domain usage updated and preserves that domains usage for all who use the internet.

ICANN issues permissions for the use of top level domains that websites use to access their content.
