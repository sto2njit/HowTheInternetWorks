PACKETS

If you want to send or receive any sort of content (messages, videos, images, etc.) the information does not necessarily just go from one computer to another; rather, the use of packets breaks up this information. The packets are sent from one computer through physical routers throughout the world (with each packet taking different routes.) Once all the packets arrive at the destination, they are remade into the desired content.

Information is sent from its origin to a specific destination broken up into packets.

TCP

The Transmission Control Protocols (TCP) verifies that all packets of information are delivered in their entirety to the desired destination. It is possible that packets can go missing, as the internet is also a physical highway of information in which data can go missing or not be delivered due to errors. In these scenarios, the TCP ensures that the packets are resent from the origin to their destination so that the packets can be fully assembled.
